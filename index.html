Fili motions ▾

For the DTW test. These record the movement of a fili, not a handshape.

  1. Pick a motion below, press Record.
  2. Hold your hand still where the motion starts — that point is the origin everything is measured from.
  3. On GO, do the motion once and stop. Speed doesn't matter — fast or slow both work. Just be decisive, and finish inside about a second.

Three takes each is enough.

Word signs ▾

Whole-word signs like salam or apple, recognised on their own — not as a fili on a letter. Unlike the fili motions these also read your handshape through the sign, so a sign that barely moves still works.

  1. Name it and type what it should output, press Add sign.
  2. Pick it, press Record, then get into the sign's starting position and hold still.
  3. On GO, sign it once and then stop. The take ends by itself when you stop moving, so a short sign is quick and a long one gets the time it needs. Nothing to press: use both hands freely.
  4. Pauses are fine. Hold as long as the sign needs; the empty time at either end is trimmed off, the hold in the middle is kept.
